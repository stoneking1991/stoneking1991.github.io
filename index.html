About

I am a 6th year PhD student in nuclear theory group, Department of Physics, University of Washington. Working with Prof. Aurel Bulgac , I study various quantum many-body problems in nuclear physics. My research primarily involves the application of density function theory (DFT) and its time-dependent extension (TDDFT) on superfluid many-fermion systems, from the static nuclear structure, to nuclear fission and reactions.

Besides expertising on nuclear physics, I have strong skills in high-performance computing, including MPI, OpenMP, and CUDA C programming. I also have a solid knowledge of applied mathematics, especially the numerical solutions of partial differential equations (PDE)

I was born in 1991 in Wuhu, Anhui, China. I got a B.S. in physics from University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) in 2013 before I joined the graduate program in University of Washington.
